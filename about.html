The story behind Lorevyn.

Why Lorevyn exists

Built out of frustration. Refined with intention.

Lorevyn started as a personal project. Like many serious readers, I had been using Goodreads for years — logging books, leaving ratings, following friends. And like many serious readers, I had grown deeply frustrated with it.

Goodreads hasn't changed meaningfully since Amazon acquired it in 2013. The interface feels like it was designed in 2008. Your reading data is used to serve Amazon recommendations. There are no real statistics about your reading patterns. Your private notes aren't private. And the experience of opening the app doesn't feel like anything at all — it's a form to fill in, not a place to spend time.

StoryGraph is a genuine improvement — built by a reader, for readers, with a clear ethical compass. But it runs on donations and goodwill. That's admirable, but it's not a stable long-term business model for something as important as your reading history.

So I built Lorevyn. A reading tracker that treats your data as private by default, not as something to harvest. An app that costs a fair amount because building something this good takes sustained effort, and sustainable products need sustainable revenue. An app that looks and feels like something worth opening every day.

The name

What does Lorevyn mean?

Lore
Old English / Latin
A body of knowledge, tradition, and learning passed through stories and books.
-vyn
Celtic / Welsh root
From "wyn" — meaning joy, blessing, or that which brings happiness.

Lorevyn means the joy of knowledge. The happiness that comes from a life spent reading. It's a made-up word — there are zero trademark conflicts and the domain was available — but its roots are real, and its meaning is exactly right.

What we stand for

Four principles that guide every decision

01

Trust is the product

Every feature decision, every privacy choice, every line of copy is evaluated against one question: does this build trust or erode it? Lorevyn exists in the intimate space of your reading life. That space deserves respect.

02

Honesty about the business

Lorevyn costs money because building something this good takes real effort. We charge a fair price and we're transparent about it. The free tier is genuinely useful because we believe in earning your upgrade, not forcing it.

03

Design that respects readers

Readers are not generic users. They are people who choose how to spend their most finite resource — their attention. Lorevyn is designed to feel worthy of that attention: beautiful, considered, and never manipulative.

04

Data belongs to the reader

Your reading history is one of the most personal records of your intellectual life. It should live where you decide, be exported whenever you want, and never be used without your knowledge. Full stop.
